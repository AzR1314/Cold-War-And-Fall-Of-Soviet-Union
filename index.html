World History Timeline

1947

Truman Doctrine

The Truman Doctrine was a U.S. policy to counter Soviet geopolitical expansion during the Cold War.

Announced by President Harry S. Truman, it provided economic and military aid to Greece and Turkey to prevent them from falling under Soviet influence, marking the start of the U.S. containment policy.

1961

Berlin Wall Erected

The Berlin Wall was built by East Germany to divide East and West Berlin.

It symbolized the Iron Curtain, preventing defections from East to West and becoming a focal point of Cold War tensions until its fall in 1989.

1962

Cuban Missile Crisis

A 13-day confrontation between the U.S. and Soviet Union over Soviet missiles in Cuba.

It was the closest the Cold War came to escalating into a full-scale nuclear war, resolved through diplomatic negotiations.

1950

McCarthyism Begins

Senator Joseph McCarthy led a campaign against alleged communists in the U.S.

His accusations sparked widespread fear, leading to investigations and blacklisting, often without solid evidence, impacting many lives.

1953

House Un-American Activities Committee (HUAC) Hearings

HUAC investigated alleged communist activities in the U.S., focusing on Hollywood and government.

The hearings led to the blacklisting of many individuals in the entertainment industry, accused of communist sympathies.

1980

Reagan Election

Ronald Reagan’s election marked the rise of conservative politics in the U.S.

His policies emphasized deregulation, tax cuts, and a strong anti-communist stance, shaping the modern conservative movement.

1981

Economic Recovery Tax Act

Reagan’s tax cut legislation aimed to stimulate economic growth.

It reduced income tax rates significantly, promoting supply-side economics but increasing federal deficits.
